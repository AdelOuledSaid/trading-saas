01

Market Regime Mapping

Reading the market by dominant theme, not local reaction

The regime commands the reading

A desk rarely begins with a setup. It begins with a larger question: what regime is currently dominant? Risk-on, risk-off, strong dollar, bond stress, sector rotation, tech expansion, safety seeking. This framing does not mechanically dictate every trade, but it structures the space of credible opportunities and the type of errors to avoid.

A bullish BTC setup in a risk-off regime demands a higher conviction threshold than the same setup in a risk-on environment. The setup may be identical — the context radically changes its expected value.

Definition

Market regime mapping is the process of identifying the dominant forces that organize the relative reading of markets at a given moment in time.

The 5 core regimes

RegimeDominant signalFavored assetsRisk appetite
ExpansionBullish momentum, clean structureEquities, BTC, growth cryptoHigh
RotationBTC dominance falling, ETH/alts gainingETH, select altsModerate
CompressionContracted volume, range behaviorNone clearly favoredLow
BreakdownStructure failing, sentiment decliningDXY, Gold, stablecoinsDefensive
TransitionConflicting signals, mixed correlationsSelective, size-reducedMinimal
Key takeaway

The same setup does not carry the same expected value across all regimes. Regime awareness is the first filter, not the last.

How to identify the current regime

  • Check US100 and S&P 500 direction and momentum on daily timeframe
  • Read BTC dominance trend — rising = BTC-led, falling = rotation phase
  • Check DXY direction — strong dollar compresses risk assets
  • Observe Fear & Greed index as a confirmation, not a trigger
  • Note which assets are leading vs lagging in the current move
Case Study — March 2024

BTC broke all-time highs while ETH lagged and most altcoins barely moved. This was a classic BTC-led Expansion regime. The correct desk response: overweight BTC, underweight alts, wait for regime confirmation before rotating. Traders who jumped into alts early suffered poor risk-adjusted returns despite a bull market.

Macro Bias & Inter-Market Logic

Connecting markets to avoid isolated reading

Macro bias does not mean predicting the economy. It means identifying relative drivers: dollar, rates, equity indices, commodities, crypto, safe-haven flows, duration pressure, risk rotation.

Inter-market logic helps you understand why certain assets become more readable, why certain ideas reinforce each other, and why others neutralize each other at the portfolio level.

The core inter-market relationships

AssetMacro rolePositive correlationNegative correlation
DXYDollar strength gaugeUSD pairsGold, BTC, emerging markets
US10YRisk-free rate pressureDXY, USDGrowth equities, BTC
GoldSafe haven & inflation hedgeDefensive assetsReal yields, DXY
US100Risk appetite barometerBTC, ETH, growth stocksDXY, VIX, Gold
BTC.DCrypto risk appetite gaugeBTC/USDETH, altcoins

What we look for

  • Dominant macro driver
  • Assets aligned with the theme
  • Assets in contradiction
  • Second-order effects
  • Divergences that signal rotation

Common mistakes

  • Taking multiple trades without seeing the common driver
  • Treating DXY as a detail
  • Confusing apparent diversification with true independence
  • Ignoring bond market signals
  • Overweighting recent correlation
Critical risk

If you are long BTC, long ETH, long SOL and long US100 at the same time, you likely have 4 expressions of a single macro bet: risk-on. This is not diversification — it is hidden concentration. One macro shift can hit all four positions simultaneously.

03

Institutional Execution Framework

Organizing a hierarchical, non-emotional execution system

Institutional execution does not mean "entering perfectly." It means executing within a repeatable framework: dominant theme, priority asset, work zone, activation conditions, appropriate size, and coherent management.

A desk trader is not evaluated only on the entry. They are evaluated on the coherence of the entire decision chain — from macro context to trade review.

The Institutional Execution Stack

Step 1 — Regime identification: what macro regime is active right now?
Step 2 — Theme selection: which macro theme is worth expressing as a trade?
Step 3 — Asset prioritization: which instrument best expresses that theme with the cleanest structure?
Step 4 — Zone identification: where is the relevant structural zone on the HTF chart?
Step 5 — Activation condition: what price action must occur before entry?
Step 6 — Size determination: what % of portfolio risk is appropriate given conviction quality?
Step 7 — Management plan: where is the invalidation? Where is the partial profit target? Where is the runner target?
Example — Execution Framework Applied

Regime: Expansion (US100 breaking highs, BTC dominance rising). Theme: crypto risk-on. Asset: BTC (cleanest structure). Zone: $60,000 HTF demand confluence. Activation: 4H close above $61,200 with volume. Size: 2% risk (high conviction). Management: invalidation below $59,500, partial at $65,000, runner to $70,000.

Liquidity Engineering

Understanding how markets attract, sweep and redistribute attention

In an institutional framework, liquidity is not just a technical detail. It becomes a language: visible zones, sweep candles, reintegrations, stop displacement, deceptive accelerations, and genuine directional recoveries.

The desk does not only try to avoid traps. It tries to understand what those traps reveal about the quality of the real move.

Liquidity layers to monitor

TypeLocationSignal when swept
Buy-side liquidityAbove visible highs, equal topsPotential short-term exhaustion or reversal
Sell-side liquidityBelow visible lows, equal bottomsPotential accumulation opportunity
Imbalance zonesLarge impulsive candle gapsHigh probability of return before continuation
Order blocksLast bullish/bearish candle before impulseInstitutional interest zone
Mitigation blocksBreaker patterns after liquidity sweepDirection confirmation post-manipulation
Critical error

Reacting to the first visible move without waiting for what the market reveals after the liquidity event. The sweep is not the trade — what happens after the sweep is the trade.

05

Scenario Tree A / B / C / D

Building a decision tree, not a single conviction

Level 4 thinking moves beyond three simple scenarios to a more nuanced decision tree: primary scenario, alternate scenario, invalidation scenario, and neutral scenario.

The goal is not to add complexity for its own sake, but to prevent mental rigidity — the most expensive cognitive failure in trading.

Scenario framework structure

Scenario A — Primary (highest probability)

  • Supported by regime, structure, and macro
  • Position sizing: full conviction (up to 2% risk)
  • Clear trigger and invalidation defined

Scenario B — Alternate (second most likely)

  • Different directional outcome or timing
  • Position sizing: reduced (0.5–1% risk)
  • Trigger: specific structural behavior

Scenario C — Invalidation

  • The condition that kills the primary thesis
  • Defines when to exit all existing positions
  • Must be precise: a level, not a feeling

Scenario D — Neutral (no position)

  • Regime is mixed or unclear
  • No actionable setup meets the threshold
  • Cash is a position — holding it is a decision
Professional insight

The best traders hold scenarios lightly. They commit to an action when the trigger fires, but they are emotionally detached from the outcome of any single scenario. They know which scenario is active at all times.

06

Portfolio Exposure Logic

The real risk lives at the portfolio level, not the trade level

Level 4 stops reasoning in unit risk alone. It reasons in allocation: how many ideas are active, how many express the same theme, which convictions deserve more exposure, which ideas should remain secondary.

Total exposure becomes a central variable — as important as the quality of any single setup.

Portfolio exposure framework

Conviction tierMax position sizeConditions
Tier A+ — Highest conviction2–3% portfolio riskAll filters aligned: regime, structure, theme, entry quality
Tier A — High conviction1–2% portfolio riskMost filters aligned, minor uncertainty on one dimension
Tier B — Moderate conviction0.5–1% portfolio riskStructural idea but weaker macro alignment
Tier C — Exploratory0.25–0.5% portfolio riskInteresting but premature — sizing for information, not profit
Hidden concentration risk

A good individual idea can become a poor portfolio decision if it adds to already saturated exposure. Before entering, ask: what is my current total exposure to this macro theme?

07

Scaling & Partial Execution

Managing conviction and confirmation with precision

Institutional execution is not necessarily full-size. It can be gradual: initial test position, reinforcement on confirmation, partial reduction at the intermediate target, residual position based on context.

This logic treats the market as a process, not a binary bet.

The three-phase scaling model

Phase 1 — Scout (25–33% of planned size): Enter at the structural zone before full confirmation. Small size allows you to be wrong without damage, while being positioned if the move develops cleanly.
Phase 2 — Core (50% of planned size): Add on confirmed activation signal — a specific candle close, a retrace, a volume confirmation. This is the conviction entry.
Phase 3 — Runner (remaining 17–25%): Optional add if the move accelerates cleanly above a key level. Only justified if the macro theme is strengthening. Never chase — this is a disciplined addition, not FOMO.
Example

A small scout position on the HTF demand zone can become a more significant conviction only if the market confirms the initial thesis. If the market immediately invalidates at entry, the scout loss is minor. If it confirms, the core and runner additions are structurally justified.

Correlation & Hidden Concentration

Detecting when multiple trades are secretly the same bet

Most traders think they are diversified when they hold 5–10 different assets. In practice, if those assets are all correlated in the same macro regime, they represent a single directional bet with 5–10 open positions.

Correlation matrix in practice

Asset pairTypical correlationRegime dependency
BTC / ETH0.85–0.95Both fall in risk-off; both rise in risk-on
BTC / US1000.6–0.8Correlated in macro stress; decouples in crypto-specific moves
Gold / DXY–0.7 to –0.9Strong inverse — avoid long Gold + long USD
BTC / DXY–0.5 to –0.75Strong dollar = headwind for BTC
SOL / ETH0.8–0.92Similar risk-on profile; slight outperformance divergence
Definition

Hidden concentration occurs when multiple open positions, despite appearing diversified by asset name, express the same underlying macro directional bet — amplifying both gains and losses beyond what any individual position sizing would suggest.

Volatility Regime & Position Sizing

Adapting exposure to market conditions in real time

Fixed position sizing is a Level 1 concept. At Level 4, position sizing becomes dynamic — it responds to the current volatility regime, the quality of the setup, and the macro environment.

Volatility-adjusted sizing model

Volatility regimeVIX / BTC realized volSize adjustmentReasoning
Low volatilityVIX below 15 / BTC vol below 40%Full size or slight increaseClean trends, reliable setups
Normal volatilityVIX 15–25 / BTC vol 40–70%Standard sizeBaseline conditions
Elevated volatilityVIX 25–35 / BTC vol 70–100%Reduce by 25–50%Wider stops needed, less reliable signals
Extreme volatilityVIX above 35 / BTC vol above 100%Reduce by 50–75% or no tradeRegime breakdown, noise dominates
Key principle

In high volatility environments, your stop loss must be wider to avoid being stopped by noise. If the stop must be wider but you keep the same position size, your actual risk per trade increases dramatically. Reduce size to maintain constant risk exposure.

The Drawdown Protocol

How institutional desks respond to losing streaks

Every professional trader will face drawdown. The question is not whether it will happen — it is whether you have a protocol for responding to it that prevents emotional decision-making from amplifying the damage.

Three-tier drawdown response

Tier 1 — Yellow alert (3–5% drawdown from equity peak): Review open positions for regime alignment. No new trades until current positions are resolved. Identify if drawdown is due to bad luck or bad process.
Tier 2 — Orange alert (5–10% drawdown): Reduce all position sizes by 50%. No new trade ideas for 48 hours. Conduct a full review of the last 10 trades. Identify the pattern causing the drawdown.
Tier 3 — Red alert (10%+ drawdown): Stop trading entirely. Full process review with an external perspective. Identify whether the market regime has shifted beyond your system's parameters. Do not resume until you can clearly articulate what changed and what you will do differently.
The revenge trading trap

The most dangerous moment in a drawdown is immediately after a loss. The psychological pressure to "make it back" is strongest exactly when your judgment is most impaired. Your protocol must be written in advance — in calm — and followed precisely when you are under pressure.

Building Your Idea Portfolio

Managing a structured book of active and pending ideas

A desk operates from a portfolio of ideas — not just from the next trade. This means maintaining a structured list of current ideas: which are active, which are pending trigger, which have been invalidated, and which are watching for regime confirmation.

Idea portfolio structure

StatusDescriptionAction
ActivePosition open, managingFollow plan, update as price evolves
Triggered — MonitoringEntry reached, watching activationSet alerts, confirm activation signal
Pending — Waiting for zoneThesis valid, price not at entry yetSet price alerts, do nothing
Watching — Regime confirmationStructure ok but macro unclearWait for regime alignment
InvalidatedStructure or macro thesis brokenRemove from list, document the reason
Practical application

At the start of each session, review your idea portfolio first — not your P&L. Identify which ideas moved toward trigger, which were invalidated, and which new regime developments change the priority ranking. This daily review replaces reactive chart-watching with structured thesis management.

Pro Review Framework

Measuring process quality, not just raw results

A professional review does not ask only whether the trade won or lost. It asks whether the process was coherent, the exposure was justified, the hierarchy was clear, and whether errors are recurring or accidental.

The pro review serves to improve the continuity of the method — not to flatter or punish the ego.

The 6-dimension review model

1. Context quality

  • Was the macro regime correctly identified?
  • Was the session timing appropriate?
  • Was there a clear macro catalyst or was it noise?

2. Thesis quality

  • Was the trade aligned with the regime?
  • Was the structural logic sound on the HTF?
  • Was the scenario clearly defined before entry?

3. Execution quality

  • Was the entry within the planned zone?
  • Was the size appropriate for conviction level?
  • Was the activation condition respected?

4. Management quality

  • Was the management plan followed?
  • Were decisions emotional or structural?
  • Was exposure reduced correctly on invalidation?

5. Portfolio coherence

  • Was total exposure within limits?
  • Was hidden concentration avoided?
  • Was the idea ranked correctly in the portfolio?

6. Transferable lesson

  • What single improvement would have changed the outcome?
  • Is this error pattern recurring?
  • What rule prevents this error in the future?

Desk Routine & Daily Protocol

Transforming advanced reading into institutional routine

The goal of Level 4 is not to produce an "impressive" trader. It is to produce an operator who can repeat a clear process: prepare, classify, rank, execute, measure, correct.

Pre-session routine (15–20 min)

  • Identify current macro regime
  • Review dominant drivers (DXY, US100, BTC.D)
  • Update idea portfolio status
  • Set maximum exposure limit for the day
  • Define scenarios by theme
  • Check economic calendar for macro events
  • Identify the one highest-conviction opportunity

Post-session routine (10–15 min)

  • Rate process quality (1–10) for each trade
  • Note position coherence with macro theme
  • Record any hidden concentration discovered
  • Update idea portfolio (active, invalidated, pending)
  • Write one transferable lesson
  • Update performance metrics
  • Set alerts for tomorrow's key levels
The compounding effect

A trader who runs this protocol for 90 consecutive trading days will have produced 90 structured reviews, 90 transferable lessons, and a deep empirical understanding of their own patterns — errors and strengths alike. This is what professional edge looks like: not a secret indicator, but a documented, evolving system.

DXY Macro Chart — Institutional Visual

Reading the dollar as a system driver at this level

At Level 4, the chart is no longer just a timing tool. It becomes a relative reading instrument. The dollar often acts as the primary driver of multiple asset classes — understanding DXY at an institutional level allows you to think about the market as a system, not a collection of individual setups.

Reading exercise

Open the DXY chart and identify the current trend on the weekly timeframe. Now check BTC, Gold, and the US100 on the same timeframe. Document whether DXY is leading, lagging, or diverging from these assets. This single exercise reveals the current macro relationship — and immediately gives your trades a structural context they would otherwise lack.
